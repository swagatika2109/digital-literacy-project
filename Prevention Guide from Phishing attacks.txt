Prevention Guide – Staying Safe from Phishing Attacks

Overview:
Phishing attacks can be prevented with awareness and simple precautionary measures. By following basic digital safety practices, students and users can significantly reduce the risk of becoming victims.

Key Safety Practices:
1. Verify Before You Trust: 
Always check the sender’s email address and message authenticity. Do not trust messages that create urgency or pressure without verification.

2. Avoid Suspicious Links:  
Do not click on unknown or shortened links received through emails or messages. Always manually type official website URLs when logging in.

3. Check Website Security:  
Ensure that websites use “https” and display a secure connection before entering any personal or financial information.

4. Protect Sensitive Information:  
Never share OTPs, passwords, or bank details with anyone, even if the request appears to come from a trusted source.

5. Use Strong Authentication:  
Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for accounts to add an extra layer of security.

6. Stay Updated:  
Keep your devices, apps, and browsers updated to protect against security vulnerabilities.

7. Be Cautious with Emails and Messages:  
Avoid downloading attachments or responding to messages from unknown senders.

8. Safe Digital Payment Practices: 
Never scan QR codes to receive money, and always verify payment requests before approving transactions.

Reporting and Support:
In case of any suspected cybercrime, users should report the incident immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the helpline number 1930 for assistance.
